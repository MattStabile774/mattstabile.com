About

Who is Matt Stabile?

World traveler, former lawyer, founder, public speaker and generally pretty nice guy — with one trait that binds all of these titles: when I set out to accomplish something, I make sure it's a success, and I have fun along the way.

Who am I in a nutshell? It's hard, but I'll give it a shot: I grew up in Colorado, graduated with a degree in Political Science from UConn, served as a White House Intern in 2002, graduated from Penn State Law School in 2006, practiced law in New York for over 7 years, founded the travel blog The Expeditioner and traveled to over 40 countries, and built my career in technical recruiting before founding Stabile Search, New York City's top quantitative finance, AI and data science recruitment firm.

What's next? I'm not sure, but if I live by one mantra, it can be summed up in this quote by Steve Jobs: "Your time is limited, don't waste it living someone else's life."

Public Speaker

An accomplished and sought-after speaker

I am an accomplished and sought-after public speaker on the topics of data science recruiting, the job search process, and hiring the best engineering talent. I have presented at numerous conferences, symposiums and meetups, and am always looking for new opportunities to speak either as an individual or as a panel member.
