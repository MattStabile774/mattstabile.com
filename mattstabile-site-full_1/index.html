FAQ

Quant finance recruiting in New York City, explained

What does a quant finance recruiter do?

A quant finance recruiter specializes in placing quantitative researchers, quant traders, quant developers and machine learning researchers at hedge funds, proprietary trading firms and asset managers. The role requires deep knowledge of how quant firms hire — from interview formats and compensation structures to non-compete dynamics and submission logistics — so both candidates and hiring managers can move quickly and confidently.

How do I find a quant finance recruiter in NYC?

Look for a recruiter with a long track record of placements at the major New York City quant funds and trading firms, direct relationships with the hiring managers themselves, and real fluency in the technical work — quant research, systematic trading, machine learning and data science. I have spent over a decade recruiting exclusively in this space here in New York City.

What roles does a quantitative finance recruiter place?

Typical placements include Quantitative Researcher, Quantitative Trader, Quantitative Developer, Machine Learning Researcher, LLM and post-training research roles, Data Scientist, and leadership positions such as Head of Quant Research or Head of Data Science. Firms range from the largest multi-strategy hedge funds and market makers to AI research labs and startups.

Do you work with candidates, companies, or both?

Both. Companies engage me to build pipelines for hard-to-fill quant, AI and data science roles in New York City. Candidates work with me confidentially to navigate the market, benchmark compensation, and get in front of the right teams at the right firms.
